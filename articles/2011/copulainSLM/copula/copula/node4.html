Where does the copula come from?

The copula is both synchronically and diachronically analyzable as conjunctive participle of the lexical verb dhaathangcome. The first Malays who arrived in Ceylon in the 176#6 century communicated in Vehicular Malay, a trade language used in and around the Indonesian archipelago (Smith, 2003, Smith et al., 2004). The language has changed considerably in the last three centuries and has typologically converged towards the local languages Tamil and Sinhala (Ansaldo, 2005, Smith & Paauw, 2006, Nordhoff, 2009, Ansaldo, 2008, Smith et al., 2004). Given these historical facts, three possible origins for the copula can be suggested: Indonesian varieties of Malay, and Sinhala or Tamil. In this section, I will discuss the copula-like structures we find in these languages and the possibility of their influence om the development of Sri Lanka Malay.
